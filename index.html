🏆 Math Competitions in North America

Explore famous mathematics competitions for students — from elementary to high school level.

🦘 International

Math Kangaroo

Grades 1–12 · March every year

The world's largest math competition with over 6 million participants in 90+ countries. Each multiple-choice problem is clever and fun — designed to spark joy in math, not just test speed. In Canada and the US, it's held every March.

Grades 1–12 Multiple Choice 75 min 30 questions
🔵 USA · MAA

AMC 8

Grades ≤ 8 · January every year

Organized by the Mathematical Association of America, AMC 8 is the entry point to the prestigious AMC/AIME/USAMO pathway. It tests logical thinking and problem-solving with 25 questions in 40 minutes. No penalty for wrong answers!

Grade ≤ 8 25 questions 40 min No penalty
📐 USA · National

MATHCOUNTS

Grades 6–8 · School → State → National

A national middle school program with four rounds: Sprint, Target, Team, and the exciting Countdown Round. Students compete at school, chapter, state, and national levels. It's fast-paced, competitive, and a rite of passage for many young mathematicians.

Grades 6–8 4 Rounds Team + Individual Nationals in May
🍁 Canada · Waterloo

Gauss Contest

Grades 7 & 8 · May every year

Run by the Centre for Education in Mathematics and Computing (CEMC) at the University of Waterloo, the Gauss Contest is a beloved Canadian tradition. Named after the legendary Carl Friedrich Gauss, it features 60-minute, 25-question multiple-choice tests. Top scorers are recognized nationally.

Grades 7–8 25 questions 60 min U of Waterloo
